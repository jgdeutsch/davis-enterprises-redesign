## Cross-Fund Pattern Analysis

### 1. HERO MESSAGING - What Appears First

| Fund | Hero Message | Pattern |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Main Sequence | "We back deep tech founders" | Identity + audience |
| Blackbird | "Enter the world of Blackbird" | Invitational / mythic |
| AirTree | "Big things / Start small" | Aspirational tagline |
| Square Peg | "We help founders from our corner of the world shape the future" | Geographic + founder-centric |
| Tenacious | "Unlocking impact at scale in agri-food systems" | Sector + mission |
| Skip Capital | "Skip Capital is a private fund investing in technology and infrastructure" | Factual one-liner |
| Lux Capital | "We turn sci-fi into sci-fact" | Bold brand statement |
| IQT | "Investing in global innovation to secure the nation" | Mission-driven |
| Breakthrough Vic | "Investing in Innovation for Victoria's Economic Future" | Geographic + institutional |

**Pattern:** 8/9 funds lead with a single, short statement (under 15 words). No fund leads with stats. The hero is always about WHO THEY ARE or WHAT THEY BELIEVE, never about fund size or returns.

### 2. LANGUAGE & TONE

**Most common characteristics (frequency across 9 funds):**

| Trait | Frequency | Examples |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Founder-centric language ("we back founders") | 7/9 | Main Sequence, Blackbird, AirTree, Square Peg, Lux, IQT, Breakthrough |
| Anti-jargon (no "synergy", "ecosystem", "alpha") | 9/9 | Universal |
| Aspirational but grounded | 7/9 | All except Skip (understated) and Tenacious (academic) |
| Short, declarative sentences | 8/9 | All except Breakthrough (institutional prose) |
| Mission-first over returns-first | 9/9 | Universal - NO fund leads with financial performance |
| Warmth / approachability | 6/9 | AirTree, Square Peg, Skip, Blackbird especially |
| Manifesto / belief-driven copy | 5/9 | Main Sequence, Blackbird, Lux, Square Peg, IQT |

**Key vocabulary patterns:**
- "Back" (not "invest in") - used by Main Sequence, Blackbird, AirTree, Square Peg, Tenacious
- "Founders" (not "companies" or "startups") - 7/9 funds center the person
- "Build" / "building" - appears on every single homepage
- "Future" / "tomorrow" - 6/9 funds use forward-looking temporal language
- "Conviction" / "belief" / "unreasonable" - 4/9 funds signal contrarian thinking

**What NO fund does:**
- Use corporate buzzwords (synergy, leverage, value-add, ecosystem)
- Lead with fund size or AUM in the hero
- Use aggressive sales language or urgency/FOMO
- Use exclamation marks
- Use emojis

### 5. HOMEPAGE STRUCTURE (Most Common Section Order)

Based on analyzing all 9 sites, the most common section flow:

1. **Navigation** (100% - all funds)
2. **Hero / identity statement** (100% - always a short, bold claim)
3. **Philosophy / thesis** (78% - how they invest or what they believe)
4. **Portfolio showcase** (89% - logos, cards, or carousel)
5. **Content / thought leadership** (67% - blog posts, investment notes, newsletters)
6. **Team** (44% - only some show team on homepage)
7. **Testimonials / social proof** (33% - surprisingly rare on homepage)
8. **Newsletter / community CTA** (56% - soft conversion)
9. **Footer** (100%)

**What is NOT on most homepages:**
- Detailed investment criteria (pushed to separate pages)
- Fund performance / IRR data (almost never public)
- Application forms (usually just "Get in Touch")
- Pricing / check sizes (rarely on homepage)
- Long "About Us" sections

### 6. TRUST SIGNALS & SOCIAL PROOF

**Most effective trust signals (ranked by frequency):**

| Trust Signal | Frequency | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Portfolio company logos / names | 9/9 | Universal - the #1 trust signal |
| Institutional backing (CSIRO, government, Atlassian) | 5/9 | Unique differentiator when available |
| Named team members | 6/9 | Humanizes the fund |
| Thought leadership content | 6/9 | Blog posts, newsletters, podcasts |
| Founder testimonials | 3/9 | Surprisingly rare (AirTree, Square Peg only heavy users) |
| Key stats (AUM, companies, unicorns) | 4/9 | When used, always understated |
| Co-investor / partner logos | 3/9 | IQT (gov agencies), AirTree (implicit), Blackbird (in articles) |
| Community programs | 3/9 | AirTree (Pioneer/Explorer), Blackbird (Sunrise), Main Sequence (Researcher Hours) |

**Most powerful trust signal for attracting startups:** Portfolio quality. Every single fund leads with their best-known portfolio companies. Founders choose investors by looking at who else those investors backed. Canva appears in 3 different fund portfolios (AirTree, Square Peg, Skip Capital).

### 7. WHAT WOULD MAKE A STARTUP CHOOSE THIS FUND?

Based on the analysis, startups from the Davis Enterprises portfolio (AI vertical SaaS, space/defense, robotics, deep tech) would look for these signals:

1. **Portfolio relevance** - "Have they backed companies like mine before?" Show companies in adjacent sectors.
2. **Founder voice** - Testimonials from real founders saying "they helped me with X specific thing"
3. **Thesis clarity** - Clear articulation of what sectors/stages they invest in
4. **Value beyond capital** - Specific examples of how they help (intros, hiring, technical advice, government access)
5. **Founder-centric tone** - Language that speaks TO founders, not ABOUT them
6. **Accessibility** - Clear CTA to start a conversation (not a 47-field application form)
7. **Track record proof** - Not just logos, but stories of how they helped companies grow
8. **Cultural fit signal** - Personality in the copy that lets founders self-select

## Summary: The Playbook for an Early-Stage Australian Deep Tech / AI Fund Website

### Must-Haves (9/9 funds do this):
- Single, bold hero statement (under 15 words)
- Portfolio showcase with company names/logos
- Anti-jargon, human language
- Mission-first positioning (not returns-first)

### Should-Haves (6-8/9 funds do this):
- Clear thesis / sector focus articulation
- Founder-centric language ("we back founders")
- Thought leadership content (blog, newsletter, investment notes)
- Named team members visible
- Soft CTA ("Let's talk" not "Apply now")

### Nice-to-Haves (3-5/9 funds do this):
- Founder testimonials
- Key stats (used sparingly)
- Community programs
- Newsletter signup

### Avoid (0/9 funds do this):
- Leading with fund size or returns
- Corporate jargon
- Aggressive sales copy
- Complex application forms on homepage
- Walls of text
- Stock photography
